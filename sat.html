SAT Reading and Writing Cheat Sheet

The digital SAT Reading and Writing section (64 minutes, 2 modules, 27 questions each) tests grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, and reading comprehension through passage-based multiple-choice questions. This cheat sheet summarizes key rules, strategies, and tips to ace the section, focusing on Standard English Conventions (grammar/punctuation) and Expression of Ideas (clarity, organization). Practice with Bluebook app tests at College Board.

Grammar and Punctuation Rules

  1. Subject-Verb Agreement
    Rule: Subject and verb must match in number (singular/plural). Ignore words between them.
    Example: The diner near the dorms, which houses students, serves breakfast. (Singular "diner" → singular "serves")
    Tip: Cross out prepositional phrases or clauses to identify the subject.
  2. Verb Tense Consistency
    Rule: Keep verbs in the same tense (present, past, future) unless there’s a clear reason to shift.
    Example: She studies hard and passes the test. (Both present tense)
    Fix: Incorrect: She studied hard but passes the test. → Correct: She studied hard and passed the test.
    Tip: Identify the established tense in the passage first.
  3. Pronoun Agreement and Clarity
    Rule: Pronouns must match their antecedent in number and gender; avoid ambiguity.
    Example: The team won its game. (Singular "team" → singular "its")
    Common Errors: Its (possessive) vs. It’s (it is); Their (plural possessive) vs. They’re (they are) vs. There (place). Who (subject, people) vs. Whom (object, people); That (things/animals).
    Tip: If a regular noun is an option, it’s often clearer than a pronoun.
  4. Possessive vs. Plural Nouns
    Rule: Possessive nouns use apostrophe + s (singular) or s + apostrophe (plural ending in s). Plural nouns ending in s use s alone.
    Example: Singular: The cat’s toy (possession). Plural: Two cats (no apostrophe). Plural not ending in s: The children’s toys.
    Fix: Incorrect: The cats’ are sleeping. → Correct: The cats are sleeping.
    Tip: Personal pronouns (e.g., hers, theirs) don’t use apostrophes.
  5. Punctuation
    Commas: Use for non-essential clauses (removable without changing meaning): Jane, who is talented, won. (Cross out: Jane won.)
    Before coordinating conjunctions (FANBOYS: For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So) linking independent clauses: The test was hard, but she passed.
    Avoid between subject and verb: Incorrect: The student, studies hard.
    No Oxford comma tested (e.g., apples, bananas, and oranges is correct).
    Semicolons: Join two independent clauses or separate list items with commas: London is old; it has history.
    Colons/Dashes: Introduce lists or explanations after a complete sentence: She brought essentials: books, pens, notes.
    Tip: Semicolons = periods; colons/dashes need a full sentence before but not after.
  6. Parallel Structure
    Rule: Items in a list or paired elements must have the same grammatical form.
    Example: She enjoys reading, writing, and jogging. (All -ing verbs)
    Fix: Incorrect: She enjoys reading, writing, and to jog. → Correct: jogging.
    Tip: Check lists for consistent noun, verb, or phrase forms.
  7. Modifiers
    Rule: Place modifiers next to the word they describe to avoid ambiguity.
    Example: Being talented, she won the award. (Modifier "being talented" clearly describes "she")
    Fix: Incorrect: Being talented, the award was given to her. → Correct: Being talented, she was given the award.
    Tip: Read without the modifier to ensure clarity.
  8. Sentence Structure
    Rule: Every sentence needs a subject and verb to form a complete thought. Avoid fragments and run-ons.
    Fragment Fix: Incorrect: Asking for a new room. → Correct: She asked for a new room.
    Run-on Fix: Incorrect: The test was hard, she passed. → Correct: The test was hard, but she passed.
    Tip: Ensure each clause has a subject and verb; connect independent clauses with a comma + FANBOYS or semicolon.
  9. Homophones and Word Choice
    Rule: Memorize common homophones: affect (verb) vs. effect (noun), insure (insurance) vs. ensure (make certain), further (abstract) vs. farther (distance).
    Example: The effect of studying is a high score. She will ensure success.
    Tip: Read carefully in passages to avoid skimming errors.
  10. Logical Comparisons
    Rule: Compare similar things logically.
    Example: Her score is higher than his (not him).
    Fix: Incorrect: Her score is higher than all students. → Correct: Her score is higher than that of all other students.
    Tip: Ensure both sides of the comparison are grammatically parallel.

Vocabulary Strategies

  • Root Words: Break down unfamiliar words using prefixes, roots, and suffixes.
    Example: "Misogynist" → mis- (hatred) + gyn (women) = hatred of women.
  • Context Clues: Use surrounding text to infer meaning (synonyms, examples, or contrasts).
  • Tip: Focus on understanding word function in passages, not just memorizing lists.

Reading Strategies

  • Passage Types: Questions cover main ideas, details, evidence, data interpretation, and word-in-context.
  • Approach:
    • Skim Blurbs: Read introductory text for context.
    • Mental Roadmap: Note main points, tone shifts, and arguments as you read.
    • Answer First: Form your own answer before checking choices to avoid traps.
    • Cross Off Wrong Answers: Eliminate clearly incorrect options to narrow choices.
  • Tip: Don’t flag every question; revisit only those needing review (use Bluebook’s flagging feature).

Time Management

  • Pace: Spend ~1 minute per question (64 minutes for 54 questions total).
  • Strategy:
    • Answer easier questions first; flag and revisit harder ones.
    • Don’t overthink; trust practice and move on if stuck.
    • Use a wristwatch to track time (no calculators or phones needed; Desmos is provided).
  • Tip: Practice with timed Bluebook tests to simulate test conditions.

General Tips

  • Practice: Use Bluebook app, Khan Academy, or resources like The Critical Reader by Erica Meltzer for realistic questions.
  • Check Context: Plug answers back into the passage to ensure they fit.
  • Avoid Informal Errors: SAT tests formal English, not conversational style (e.g., “they” is plural, not singular).

Quick Reference Checklist

  • Before Test: Memorize rules, practice with Desmos (see shortcuts below), and take timed practice tests.
  • During Test: Read passages lightly, focus on underlined sections, eliminate wrong answers, and verify with context.
  • Common Traps: Watch for homophones, misplaced modifiers, and comma splices.

Algebra

Key Properties & Formulas

  • Zero Product Property: If ab = 0, then a = 0 or b = 0.

Systems of Equations

  • One solution: lines intersect at one point (different slopes).
  • No solution: parallel lines (same slope, different intercepts).
  • Infinite solutions: same line (same slope and intercept).

Slope, Midpoint, and Distance

  • Slope Formula: m = (y₂ - y₁)/(x₂ - x₁)
  • Slope-Intercept Form: y = mx + b
  • Midpoint Formula: ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2)
  • Distance Formula: d = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]

Algebra

Exponents & Radicals

  • Exponents: repeated multiplication. Example: 2³ = 8
  • Radicals: roots. Example: √x = x1/2
  • Key Laws: product, quotient, power, negative, zero, fractional exponents.

  • am/n = n√am
  • √ab = √a · √b

    Quadratics

    • Vertex form: y = a(x - h)² + k, vertex (h, k)
    • Factored form: y = a(x - r₁)(x - r₂)
    • Standard form: y = ax² + bx + c
    • Vertex: x = -b/(2a)
    • Quadratic Formula: x = [-b ± √(b²-4ac)]/(2a)
    • Discriminant: b² - 4ac (determines number of real roots)

    Functions & Their Graphs

    • Functions map inputs to outputs: f(x) = 2x + 3
    • Composite: f(g(x))
    • Visualize linear, quadratic, and exponential functions on a graph.
    Mnemonic: "Rise over run" for slope; "y = mx + b" for lines.
    Q: If f(x) = 2x + 3, find f(4).
    A: 11

    Expressions

    • Combine like terms, expand, and factor.
    • Use identities: (a+b)2 = a2 + 2ab + b2
    Mnemonic: "FOIL" for binomial expansion: First, Outer, Inner, Last.
    Q: Expand (x+2)2 .
    A: x2 + 4x + 4

    Equations

    • Solve linear and quadratic equations.
    • Quadratic formula: x = [-b ± √(b²-4ac)]/(2a)
    Mnemonic: "x equals negative b, plus or minus the square root, of b squared minus 4ac, all over 2a."
    Q: Solve 2x/3 = 4/5.
    A: x = 6/5

    Absolute Value

    • Distance from zero: |x| ≥ 0
    • Solve: |x| < a ⇒ -a < x < a
    Q: Solve |x - 2| = 10.
    A: x = 12, -8

    Geometry & Circles

    Circle & Sector Formulas

    • Circle Equation: (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²
    • Arc Length (degrees): θ/360 = L/(2πr)
    • Arc Length (radians): L = rθ
    • Sector Area (degrees): θ/360 = A/(πr²)
    • Sector Area (radians): A = (1/2)r²θ

    Geometry

    Lines

    • Slope: m = (y₂ - y₁)/(x₂ - x₁)
    • Slope-intercept: y = mx + b
    • Point-slope: y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)
    Q: Find the equation of a line through (6,8) with slope 1/3.
    A: y = (1/3)x + 6

    Triangles

    • Sum of angles: 180°
    • Pythagorean theorem: a² + b² = c²
    • Special triangles: 45-45-90 (x, x, x√2), 30-60-90 (x, x√3, 2x)
    Mnemonic: "1, 1, √2" for 45-45-90; "1, √3, 2" for 30-60-90.
    A: 10

    Circles

    • Area: πr²
    • Circumference: 2πr
    • Equation: (x-h)² + (y-k)² = r²
    Q: Find the area of a circle with radius 3.
    A: 9π

    Polygons & Volume

    • Sum of interior angles: 180(n-2)
    • Volume: Cube (s³), Prism (lwh), Cylinder (πr²h), Cone ((1/3)πr²h), Sphere ((4/3)πr³)
    Q: Find the volume of a cylinder with radius 2 and height 5.
    A: 20π

    Trigonometry

    SOHCAHTOA

    • sinθ = opposite/hypotenuse
    • cosθ = adjacent/hypotenuse
    • tanθ = opposite/adjacent

    Trigonometry

    • Sine, cosine, tangent: SOH-CAH-TOA
    • Complementary angles: sin x = cos(90° - x)
    • Key values: sin 30° = 1/2, cos 45° = √2/2
    Mnemonic: SOH-CAH-TOA (Sine = Opp/Hyp, Cos = Adj/Hyp, Tan = Opp/Adj)
    Q: In a right triangle with legs 3 and 4, find sin θ for the angle opposite 3.
    A: 3/5

    Statistics

    Key Statistics

    • Mean: sum of terms / number of terms
    • Median: middle value
    • Mode: most frequent value
    • Line of Best Fit: Approximates data trend in scatterplots

    Statistics & Probability

    Probability

    • Probability = favorable outcomes / total outcomes
    Q: A bag has 5 red and 3 blue marbles. Probability of red?
    A: 5/8

    Statistics

    • Mean: average
    • Median: middle value
    • Mode: most frequent
    • Range: max - min
    Q: Find mean, median, mode, range of 2, 3, 3, 4, 5.
    A: Mean = 3.4, Median = 3, Mode = 3, Range = 3

    Graph Behavior & Transformations

    Key Properties

    • Linear Graphs: y = mx + b is a straight line with slope m and y-intercept b. If m > 0, line slopes up; if m < 0, slopes down.
    • Quadratic Graphs: y = ax² + bx + c is a parabola. Opens up if a > 0, down if a < 0. Vertex is min or max point.
    • End Behavior (Polynomials):
      • Even degree: positive leading coefficient, both ends up (∪); negative, both ends down (∩).
      • Odd degree: positive leading coefficient, left down/right up; negative, left up/right down.
    • Transformations:
      • Horizontal Shift: y = f(x - h) shifts right by h.
      • Vertical Shift: y = f(x) + k shifts up by k.

    Other Topics

    Percent & Growth

    • Linear: y = mt + b; Exponential: y = ab^t
    Q: Price increases from $20 to $25. Percent increase?
    A: 25%

    Rates & Radians

    • Speed = distance/time
    • Degrees to radians: degrees × π/180
    Q: Convert 90° to radians.
    A: π/2

    • Inequalities: Type y > 2x + 3; Desmos shades solution region; overlap for systems.
    • Sliders: Add sliders for variables (e.g., y = ax + 3) to test transformations or no-solution cases.

    SAT Math-Style Questions & How to Use Desmos

    Practice these representative SAT math questions and see exactly how to use Desmos to solve each one on test day.

    1. Linear Equations & Functions
      • Q1. Solve for x: 2x + 3 = 11.
        Desmos: Type y = 2x + 3 and y = 11. The intersection x-value is the solution.
      • Q2. Find x where 3x - 5 = 4x + 2.
        Desmos: Type y = 3x - 5 and y = 4x + 2. The intersection x-coordinate is your answer.
      • Q3. A line passes through (2, 3) and (4, 7). Find its equation.
        Desmos: Add points (2,3) and (4,7). Use the slope formula or y1 ~ mx1 + b regression in Desmos.
    2. Systems of Equations
      • Q4. Solve the system: 2x + y = 8, x - y = 1.
        Desmos: Enter both equations (e.g., y = 8 - 2x and y = x - 1). The intersection point gives (x, y).
    3. Quadratic Equations
      • Q5. Solve x² - 5x + 6 = 0.
        Desmos: Type y = x^2 - 5x + 6. The x-intercepts are the solutions.
      • Q6. A ball is thrown: h = -16t² + 32t + 48. When does it hit the ground?
        Desmos: Graph y = -16t^2 + 32t + 48. Find where the curve crosses the x-axis.
    4. Inequalities
      • Q7. Solve x + 4 > 7.
        Desmos: Graph y = x + 4 and y = 7. Check where x + 4 is above 7.
      • Q8. Which x-values satisfy both y < 2x + 3 and y > -x + 1?
        Desmos: Enter both inequalities. The overlapping shaded region is the solution set.
    5. Word Problems & Linear Models
      • Q9. A taxi charges $3 plus $2 per mile. Find the cost for 5 miles.
        Desmos: Graph y = 3 + 2x. Use the table to find y when x = 5.
    6. Geometry & Circles
      • Q10. Circle: (x-2)² + (y+1)² = 16. Find center and radius.
        Desmos: Graph the equation. Center is (2, -1), radius is 4.
      • Q11. Find intersection points of x² + y² = 25 and y = x.
        Desmos: Enter both equations. Click intersection points.
    7. Exponents and Roots
      • Q12. Solve 2^x = 8.
        Desmos: Graph y = 2^x and y = 8. Intersection x-value is the answer.
      • Q13. Solve sqrt(x + 4) = 5.
        Desmos: Graph y = sqrt(x + 4) and y = 5. Intersection x-value is the solution.
    8. Ratios & Proportions
      • Q14. If 3 pens cost $6, how much do 7 pens cost?
        Desmos: Use a table: x = 3, y = 6, then find y for x = 7. Or graph y = 2x.
    9. Statistics
      • Q15. Find the line of best fit for (1,2), (2,3), (3,5), (4,7).
        Desmos: Use the table, then type y1 ~ mx1 + b for regression.
      • Q16. 5 numbers have mean 10. Four are 8, 9, 12, 11. Find the missing number.
        Desmos: Sum known values, use (sum + x)/5 = 10. Solve for x.
    10. Trigonometry (Basic)
      • Q17. Solve for x: sin x = 0.5, 0 ≤ x ≤ 180°.
        Desmos: Graph y = sin(x) (degrees mode) and y = 0.5. Intersection points are solutions.
      • Q18. Find the height of a building if angle of elevation is 30° from 20m away.
        Desmos: Use tan(30°) = height / 20. Graph y = tan(30°) * x.
    11. Complex Problems
      • Q19. Parabola y = x² - 4x + 3 and line y = x - 1 intersect. Find points.
        Desmos: Graph both. Click intersection points.
      • Q20. Maximize area of rectangle with perimeter 20.
        Desmos: Express area A = x(10 - x). Graph and find maximum.
