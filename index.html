Financial Aid & Scholarships

Securing financial aid is your top priority. This section explains the critical difference between university aid policies and highlights key scholarships. Understanding these concepts is essential to building a realistic and successful application strategy.

Need-Blind ✅

These universities do NOT consider your ability to pay when making an admission decision. This is the gold standard for aid seekers.

Schools include: Harvard, Yale, Princeton, MIT, Amherst, Bowdoin, Dartmouth, Williams.

Need-Aware / 100% Need Met ⚠️

These schools DO consider your need for aid in their admission decision. However, if they admit you, they promise to cover 100% of your calculated need. Your application must be exceptionally strong to be chosen.

Key Forms & External Scholarships

  • CSS Profile

    The most common financial aid application for private universities. It's more detailed than other forms. Requires financial documents like bank statements and income records.

  • ISFAA (International Student Financial Aid Application)

    A free alternative to the CSS Profile used by some universities. Check each school's requirements.

  • External Scholarships

    Don't rely solely on universities. Actively apply to external scholarships. Top options for Kenyan students include the Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program and Zawadi Africa Education Fund.

Essays & Activities

Your essays and activities list are where you tell your unique story. This is your chance to go beyond grades and scores. Use this space to connect your experiences in Kenya—from the swim team to volunteering—to your ambitions in the tech world.

Essay Strategy

Your main essay should reveal your character. Don't just write about *what* you did; write about *why* it mattered and how it changed you.

  • Prompt Idea: Reflect on a time when you faced a challenge. How did your discipline from swimming or orchestra help you solve it?
  • "Why Us?" Essay: Research specific professors, courses, or labs at each university. Connect them to your specific interests in CS.
  • Show, Don't Tell: Instead of saying you are "hardworking," describe a time you worked for weeks on a difficult coding problem.

Framing Your Activities

Describe your extracurriculars using action verbs and quantifiable results when possible.

  • Swim Team: "Competed in national-level meets; trained 15 hours/week, fostering discipline and time management."
  • Orchestra: "First-chair violinist; collaborated with 40+ musicians to perform complex classical pieces."
  • Volunteering: "Organized a local coding club for 20 younger students, teaching basic Python and web design."

Testing & Academics

This section details how to handle the standardized requirements of U.S. applications, from the SAT to your KCSE transcript. Presenting your academic profile correctly is a foundational step for a successful application.

Standardized Testing Plan

While many schools are test-optional, a strong SAT score is a significant advantage for international students seeking aid. Plan to take it at least once.

  • Which Test?: The Digital SAT is generally recommended for its shorter length and adaptive format.
  • Preparation: Use the free, official practice materials on Khan Academy and the College Board's Bluebook app. This is the most effective way to prepare. For a deep dive, visit the SAT Prep Hub.
  • Registration: Register for the October or December 2025 tests via the College Board website. Register early to find a spot in a Kenyan test center.
  • English Proficiency: As you were educated in English, many universities will waive the TOEFL/IELTS requirement. Always check each school's specific policy. If required, the Duolingo English Test is often a cheaper and more convenient option.

Kenyan Academic Records

Your KCSE results are the centerpiece of your academic record. Here is how to prepare them:

  • Required Documents: You will need your official KCSE certificate and your high school transcripts (Form 1-4 records).
  • GPA Conversion: Do NOT try to convert your KCSE grades to a 4.0 GPA yourself. U.S. admissions officers are trained to evaluate the Kenyan system. Provide your grades as they are.
  • School Profile: Ask your high school counselor to provide a "school profile"—a document that describes your school, its curriculum, and its grading scale. This provides crucial context for admissions officers.

Math: The Formula & Concept Toolkit

Heart of Algebra

Linear Equations: $y = mx + b$

Slope Formula: $m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$

Systems of Equations: Master substitution and elimination methods.

Problem Solving & Data Analysis

Ratios, Proportions, Percentages: $\frac{part}{whole} = \frac{\%}{100}$

Mean (Average): $\frac{\text{sum of terms}}{\text{number of terms}}$

Probability: $\frac{\text{number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{total number of outcomes}}$

Passport to Advanced Math

Quadratic Formula: $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$

Vertex Form: $y = a(x-h)^2 + k$

Exponent Rules: $x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}$ and $(x^a)^b = x^{ab}$

Difference of Squares: $x^2 - y^2 = (x+y)(x-y)$

Geometry & Trigonometry

Pythagorean Theorem: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$

Area of a Circle: $A = \pi r^2$

Circumference: $C = 2\pi r$

Volume of a Cylinder: $V = \pi r^2 h$

SOHCAHTOA: $\sin(x) = \frac{O}{H}$, $\cos(x) = \frac{A}{H}$, $\tan(x) = \frac{O}{A}$

Degrees to Radians: $degrees \cdot \frac{\pi}{180}$

Reading & Writing: Strategies for Perfection

  • Read the Question First: Know what you're looking for before you read the passage. This makes your reading active and purposeful.
  • Find Direct Evidence: Every correct answer is directly supported by the text. You should be able to point to the line or phrase that proves your answer. Avoid making inferences.
  • Master Punctuation: Understand the roles of commas, semicolons, colons, and dashes. A semicolon (;) connects two complete sentences. A colon (:) introduces a list or explanation.
  • Subject-Verb Agreement: Find the true subject of the sentence and ensure the verb matches it in number (singular/plural), ignoring intervening phrases.
  • Logical Transitions: Read the sentences before and after a transition word. Determine the relationship: is it continuation (e.g., *moreover*), contrast (e.g., *however*), or cause-and-effect (e.g., *therefore*)?
  • Eliminate, Don't Select: It's often easier to find flaws in the three wrong answers than to confirm the right one. Actively look for answers that are too extreme, off-topic, or only partially true.

F-1 Visa Process (Post-Acceptance)

  1. Receive Form I-20 from your university.
  2. Pay the SEVIS I-901 Fee online.
  3. Complete the DS-160 online visa application.
  4. Schedule an interview at the U.S. Embassy in Nairobi.
  5. Gather documents: I-20, passport, financial proof, acceptance letter.
  6. Attend your interview and answer questions confidently.
